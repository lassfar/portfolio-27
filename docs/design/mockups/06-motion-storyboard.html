Hero · the star

a quiet maker of small universes

Entrance

On load: black screen → faint stars fade in (stagger) → the star core ignites (scale 0→1 + bloom rising) → nebula shells bloom outward with a slow rotation → diffraction spikes flicker in. ~1.5s, ease power3.out.

3D

R3F The centerpiece. Emissive sphere + Bloom post-processing for the glow; a noise (fbm) shader for the nebula gas; the star drifts subtly toward the cursor and shimmers on a time uniform when idle.

Text

Eyebrow "I'm Aymane —" fades up. Headline (Great Vibes) writes in character-by-character via SplitText. Sub-line fades up after. "scroll to wander ↓" gently bobs on a loop.

Scroll

As you scroll away, the star shrinks and drifts upward, nebula fades — it recedes into the distance.

Hand-off

The star becomes just another point of light in the background starfield as the planet rises into view.

About · the planet

Beyond the code

Entrance

On scroll-in: the planet scales up from small while rotating in; the ring draws itself (stroke dash); the atmosphere rim glows on. Then title + paragraphs reveal.

3D

R3F Planet = textured sphere (color + normal map) on a slow auto-rotation; ring = a thin torus; a fresnel shell for the atmosphere glow. Rotation speed can nudge with scroll velocity.

Text

Title writes in (SplitText). The two paragraphs fade + rise on enter, staggered line by line. Planet sits centered above the title.

Scroll

Planet parallaxes slower than the text (depth) and keeps gently rotating as you pass.

Hand-off

Planet drifts off-screen as the camera continues into deeper space toward the constellation.

What I'm drawn to · constellation

A camera in the stars

Entrance

Stars pop in one by one (scale + twinkle), then connector lines draw between them (stroke-dashoffset) until the camera shape forms. Labels fade in at their stars last.

3D

CSS / SVG now Crisp SVG line-draw is best here; optional light 3D parallax giving stars different depths later. Stars twinkle on an opacity loop; hovering a star highlights its label.

Text

Intro line writes in. Skill labels (React, Three.js, GSAP…) fade + scale in at their star positions.

Scroll

Signature scrub: the lines draw in time with your scroll — scroll down and the camera assembles itself; scroll up and it comes apart.

Hand-off

Constellation drifts back into the field; the camera continues toward the little worlds.

Light I've caught · the worlds

Each photo, a world

Entrance

Worlds drift in from depth (scale + fade), staggered, then settle into a gentle continuous float (each on its own y-bob phase).

3D

R3F Strongest interactive moment: each world = a textured sphere, slowly rotating + floating. Hover → the world expands and opens into the photograph (texture/plane reveal). Landscape shots map naturally onto planet surfaces.

Text

Lead line "I photograph the sky when I'm not coding it" writes in; body fades up.

Scroll

Worlds parallax at different speeds (depth) and drift horizontally as you scroll — like passing planets.

Hand-off

Worlds drift out into the dark as you approach the Lab.

Contact · the galaxy

Say hello

Entrance

The galaxy fades and rotates into view, then keeps slowly spinning; a shooting star streaks across on a loop with a random delay. Title writes in; form fields fade up staggered; the CTA softly pulses.

3D

R3F Closing showpiece: galaxy = particle system (thousands of points on spiral arms) slowly rotating with a bloomed core. The shooting star is a moving particle.

Text

"Say hello" writes in; invitation fades up. Input focus adds a soft glow.

Scroll

The galaxy is the destination — scroll settles here; it keeps living (spinning) while you read.

Micro-interaction

On submit: "Send it into orbit" — the message launches as a shooting star arcing into the galaxy. A tiny, delightful ending.

How it starts & ends

Start: a black, silent screen. One star is born — your star — and ignites with light. Your name writes itself beneath it, and a quiet line invites: scroll to wander.

End: you arrive at a whole galaxy, slowly turning, and send a message that flies off as a shooting star into the dark.

The bookend: the journey begins with a single star and ends inside a galaxy — from one light to many. From a quiet person alone, to a connection made across the dark. That arc is the story.
