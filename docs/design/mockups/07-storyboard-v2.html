Text

Scroll ✓

0→0.2: content exits (yPercent −60 + fade). 0.24→0.48: star centers on screen. 0.24→0.62: star zooms continuously (ease-in) until visible height = 100vh exactly. How it starts & ends

Start: A black, silent screen. One star is born — your star — and ignites with light. Your name writes itself beneath it, and a quiet line invites: scroll to wander ↓.

The explosion: The star doesn't die. It detonates at full screen, releases itself, and the same particles that built it drift inward and become something denser — your planet, your inner world. Same material. Different form.

The Sun: The settled core that survived the explosion becomes the Sun at the center of your photography section. It is you — not raw and burning, but calm and gravitational. Nine worlds you caught revolve around it.

End: You arrive at a whole galaxy — slowly turning — and send a message that flies off as a shooting star into the dark.

The arc: From a single point of raw light to a whole cosmos. From making things alone to connection across the dark. One material, many forms. That is the story.
